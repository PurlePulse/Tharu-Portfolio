About Me

Passionate Performer, Teacher & Visual Artist

Tharushika Divyanjali is a dynamic student at the University of the Visual and Performing Arts, immersed in the captivating worlds of dance, drama, and visual artistry. A dedicated performer, she has actively participated in numerous stage productions, embracing roles that challenge and inspire. Her passion extends to ballet and contemporary dance, where she finds grace and expression through movement, showcasing versatility in modern movement and theatrical expression.

Beyond performance, Tharushika serves as Assistant Teacher at Kurunegala Ballet Academy in Sri Lanka, where she shares her expertise in classical ballet with the next generation of dancers. This teaching role allows her to contribute to the preservation and transmission of ballet technique while developing her pedagogical skills. Her experience as both performer and teacher demonstrates a comprehensive commitment to the art of dance.

Whether on stage, in the studio teaching, or crafting visual pieces, Tharushika seeks to create art that resonates with audiences and pushes creative boundaries.
